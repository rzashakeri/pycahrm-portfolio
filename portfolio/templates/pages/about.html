My name is Reza Shakeri, and I began coding when I was 13 years old. In the same year, I started a blog about animals called animalsiran.mianblog.com. There, I became acquainted with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, and I customization of this blog. This was not the end of the story, since at the end of the same year, I created an Android app called Iran's animals and launched it on Cafe Bazar and Myket Market, which was about Iran's animals. After that, I began learning Python, gained experience with and used various hacking and security tools, and began learning penetration testing. I remained in this field for a while, learning various things. After that, I proceeded to WordPress website design and produced my first website, WordPress Magazine, which was about WordPress and related tutorials. For numerous years, I worked with WordPress. I enrolled at university, where they taught us C++. At the same time, I launched an Instagram community called barnamenevisiinsta, which grew to become one of the first and largest Persian communities of Iranian programmers, and I was providing programming material for it. I saw that it was my turn to create my own system, so I learned C# and created several Windows programs such as accounting applications, libraries, and calculators, and I started making games with unity and also created a 2D game, but the purpose of learning C# was to learn the ASP.NET Core framework, why? Because I had heard about establishing Stack Overflow using this framework, I learnt it and built a blog and an online store with it, but I couldn't interact with it for different reasons, and that was the end of my use of ASP.NET Core. I went to learn Django, and the process was and still is really enjoyable for me. I used Django to create a blog and eventually an online store. However, this was insufficient because Django was more capable. So, what exactly did I do? I used it to create a social network called Happier, however I discovered that there is a difficulty validating the files supplied by the user. The Happier project was put on hold, and I wrote a new library called django audio validator to handle the problem of validating files, but it had a major flaw: it only validated audio files. In this case, I wrote a new library called file validator, which validated all types of files and was well-tested and documented. Meanwhile, there was a major issue with publishing my content to Instagram, and that was the presence of rigorous filtering in the Islamic Republic, which made it difficult to upload my posts to Instagram. I decided to create a Telegram bot to upload my posts to Instagram via Telegram, and the outcome was Instagram-Assistant. In addition to uploading, I added other nice features like a lottery between likes and post comments, downloading any form of Instagram media, obtaining post statistics, and so on. This story continues, and I am learning new things and teaching them to others because this is how we learn best. I am also a student of computer engineering.
